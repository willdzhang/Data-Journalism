Less Activity with Higher Poverty?

In this D3 Chart, I explored the association between poverty and physical activity. Data was collected from the American Community Survey (ACS) and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) for 2014. Each marker represents an individual state in the U.S. As the chart indicates, states with less poverty seem to have high physical activity than states with higher poverty. Mississippi is the state with 21.5% of the population in poverty and has the least physically active percentage of people with only 68.4%, wheras states like Colorado and New Hampshire have poverty rates of less than or equal to 12% and have over 80% of their population considered physcially active.
